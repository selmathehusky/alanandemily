San Francisco

Staying in San Francisco is a great option as well. The meadery is about 1 hour from the city and the drive takes you across the beautiful Golden Gate Bridge, through rolling hills, and redwood forests. There are many options in the city, please let us know if you need help with recommendations.

Transportation

The meadery has parking available to those who choose to drive. If you are driving and would like to offer a seat to another guest, feel free to let us know and we'll help arrange it.

Please let us know if you would like to participate in a shuttle (no drunk driving) for transportation so we can arrange convenient pick up and drop off points. Check back for updates!

Things to do

Point Reyes

The area surrounding Point Reyes is full of amazing things to do from beautiful hikes to world class food and drinks. Here's a small list of things Emily and I recommend checking out:

Hiking - Large range of difficulty. The Earthquake trail is a very short but interesting walk from the visitor center, you can see how far the tectonic plates moved in the great 1906 earthquake. Tomales Point Trail is a longer but great hike where you will see lots of elk. Other noteworthy spots include Muddy Hollow Trail, , and Point Reyes Lighthouse.

The Marshall Store - Great place for food! It's a sweet seafood shack offering oysters & other local shellfish, smoked meats by the pound & outdoor tables.

Hog Island Osyter Co. - If you're looking for more oysters, reserve a table here and shuck and bbq your own oysters. Lots of fun with family and friends.

Lagunitas Brewing - Brewery located about 25 minutes from Point Reyes in Petaluma. This is a great place to hang out.

Petaluma Pie Company - If you find yourself in Petaluma, check out this small pie shop and treat yourself to a sourcream apple pie. Delicious!

Stinson Beach - Who doesn't like the beach? Parking fills up so try to get there early. Other beaches include Limantour Beach and Bolinas Beach.

Muir Woods - Gotta check out the beautiful redwood trees.
